Part I: Overcoming Procrastination

Do it Today, Not Tomorrow

It is easy to do the easier jobs escaping the hard reality or necessary jobs. It is the tendency of the procrastination – being comfortable, finding easy jobs to escape the actual jobs. It is not that you do not know what to do, it is just that you are not doing, and it is a constant struggle with inner self. Some ways to overcome the challenges –

1.        Exercise mental toughness everyday – Read about stoicism, pragmatism, mindfulness, etc.

2.        Exercise body everyday

3.        Set up daily habits that helps you stay in control

4.        Make a list of small tasks but important tasks that should be completed

5.        Study and practice the science of persuasion

Bottomline is to build a sustainable system for life. Whatever it is, put to action today.

Book reference: The War of Art

“Resistance is always lying and is always full of shit”.

What I Do When I Can’t Focus

We can’t focus because we are distracted. We need serotonin (happy chemical) to be released constantly by brain. That’s why we can’t focus on one thing as serotonin levels reduce with time – seeking distractions that produce the happy chemical. There are two ways to stay focused, 1) Eliminate all the useless things and, 2) think about the past success or happy moments. Make life easy by eliminating unnecessary things – Focus on what matters to you. Don’t get distracted. Stay on path.

How to beat Procrastination

“You cannot escape the responsibility of tomorrow by evading it today” – Abraham Lincoln

The cost of procrastination, as published by Dianne Tice and Roy Baumeister, is related to Depression, Irrational beliefs, Low self-esteem, Anxiety and Stress. Procrastinators, depend on external pressure [imminent deadlines] for them to work. They waste all the time for the force to act. This is just poor self-regulation.

Nobody likes it, but most of the people do it. It is because of poor self-regulation. You never get anything done because distractions, negative self-talk, other ideas, failure, other opportunities, had come in the way. Another attitude of procrastinators is “it can wait for later.”

The solution is: Don’t depend on will power rather depend on frameworks, systems. Build a routine. Some things that research proved effective include self-imposed deadlines, Accountability systems, working/studying in intervals, exercising for 30 minutes, A healthy diet, eliminating distractions and importantly the internal motivation – why do you do what you do?  

How to stop wasting time and improve effectiveness

It is easy to do useless work. Work that doesn’t bring closer to your desired outcomes. To be effective and stop procrastinating we must keep track of the time (implies we value our time). There are three things to stop wasting time and improve effectiveness. They are: 1) Know Thy time – keep an hourly record of everything you do, not daily but at a stretch of 2-3 weeks and twice or thrice in a year. 2) Identify the Non – Productive Work – From the record identify the work that is not contributing to the desired goals. 3) Eliminate the time wasters – Finally, all this exercise leads to actions. That is to eliminate the time wasters.

These time wasters enter everyday life unknowingly and become habits which will be difficult to get rid of. To be effective, eliminate the time wasters.

Book reference: The Effective Executive by Peter Drucker.

 

The habits of Unproductive people you don’t want to copy

We are often busy doing unproductive work, just like the time wasters above. Top ten unproductive habits that people have are:

1)        Working too much: Don’t work too much, that will lead to fatigue, crash, or burnout quickly. Be patient, easy on yourself and keep an eye on deadlines. Everybody wants things to be done fast but know your deadlines.

2)        Worrying too much: “What if” this, that, then… Don’t get your head so far in the sand like an ostrich that you can’t see how self-absorbed that way of thinking is. Learn to stop worrying – you are not going to die now; get up and start working.  

3)        Being Stubborn: It is okay to be stubborn with the people who only criticize by not listening to them. However, being stubborn in relationships is frustrating. We do not know everything, and it is okay to listening to others sometimes. Or we will find out – give a chance.

4)        Checking Things: Just checking Facebook, Instagram or anything is a bad habit and is a big timewaster. Although it is difficult to eliminate checking as we have personal favorites like checking cricket score, friends’ messages, etc. We can minimize the time wasters.

5)        Escaping Life: Escaping problems or pretending they don’t exist is not the solution but to tackle them early on will save time and save you from mental pressure. Problems become big problems, so solve it early. Unproductive people escape life.

6)        Saying yes: Saying no is hard. However, saying yes to million things is worse than saying no. To be productive and effective, it is essential to say no everything that we come across while we filter the most important things that contribute to our desired goals.

7)        Not writing things down: Writing things will help your brain by not remembering everything. We can use this brain power to do productive work or solve the challenges. It is easy to say that I can remember this, when we forget it takes a great deal to remember or there is a danger that we forget. Help your brain by writing things down.

8)        Being Hard on yourself: No, you don’t suck. Come on let’s get to work.

9)        Neglecting your personal education: It is important to learn something every day. This will improve your productivity and help move up in the career. Not learning new things will leave you at the bottom after some time.

10)  Hating rules: Rules are, although irritating in the first, essential to keep up the good work. Without rules, everything will turn into a colossal waste of time.

Therefore, make up rules, leave the useless things.

 

This 30-Minute evening ritual will help you to kick life in the ass

It is important to have a productive day. However, without a good night last sleep, there is an uneasiness, or the body demands rest eventually messing up with productivity and effectiveness. Therefore, wind down the day, go to sleep peacefully and wake up confidently. For that there is a 30-minute evening ritual, where first 10 minutes is dedicated to recapping the day – which should make you applaud or proud, the next ten minutes are dedicated to looking at your tomorrow’s schedule – decide on handling pressing needs, next 5 minutes are to prepare your outfit – decisions consume brain power and reduce a decision for tomorrow by deciding the outfit today, and finally the last 5 minutes are to visualize tomorrow – all successful people visualize their tomorrow and most productive people visualize with more specificity; why not we adapt this?

Sleep without stress; wake up with focus!

Why disconnecting from the internet improves your focus

Seneca said, “Being everywhere is being nowhere”. Internet takes us everywhere that means we are nowhere. Internet applications are in general made to use us/ our time. However, it is wise to use the internet rather than be used by. Therefore, eliminating internet for useless things is important and is a part of eliminate distractions. Instead of always on “Mobile net” or “WIFI”, make it always off. Turn it on only when it is needed. That way we use the internet rather than internet uses us. We overuse internet because there is no visible repercussions. Few good examples are, we do not take too much food, because it is unhealthy. We do not over gym because we will be overtrained. Similarly, there is no sentence completion for we do not overuse internet because…? It should come from self-control or self-realization. It is like there is always food, but we do not want to eat. Eat only when you are hungry.

 

The single biggest reason most people procrastinate in life

Is that you are not doing what you are passionate about! That’s it. Life is too short to waste on work that is not meaningful to you or that you don’t enjoy.

We all sorely complain about the shortness of time, and yet we have much more than we know what to do with. Our lives are either spent in doing nothing at all or doing nothing to the purpose or in doing nothing that we ought to do. We are always complaining that our days are few and acting as if there would be no end of them. -Seneca

You may delay but time may not. – Benjamin Franklin.

This Ancient Habit will focus maximize your focus

We all worry about things that will never happen. And most of the times these are the things that keeps us occupied. However, to focus, to be productive we need to work. And how do we shift from “worry endlessly” to working towards our goal? One ancient habit is to have a mantra that will shift focus. “Hard work. Dedication.” By Floyd Mayweather, “Let’s go” by this author (Darius) by repeating this time and again, we remind ourselves that we need to work towards our goal, be positive, and worrying endlessly will fetch nothing. Pessimists say saying a mantra will not change anything. However, having a mantra implies we actively remind ourselves that we are more than worrying! Be positive!

How to Focus better: Manage your attention not your time

In this modern day, we have so many distractions be in on mobile or with people. It is not the new problem that we face, it is human nature to be distracted by trivial things. If we are working on something, it is natural to drift away and it is very rare that we get enough time to focus on what we want. If we could manage our attention, without distracting ourselves, we could be productive, content and work towards our desired goal.

Part II: Improving Productivity

Smartphones harm your productivity more than you think

There is a grammar mistake in page 51 (last but one paragraph) - The sentence starts with first person and ends with second person.

Last page of the chapter – “They want to consume their content, on you guessed it, your smartphone”. I think the comma should be after on.

There is a study that showed productivity improves by 26% when people are away from their mobile phones. It is not absolute, but it works. There are always notifications saying that “We think you might like it”, or “Something happened: Just kidding”, “we missed you for a while”, etc.  They are just distractions. Mute the notifications on unnecessary apps and see the magic.

How perfectionism is destroying your productivity

Perfectionism is good, you achieve great things. But in the name of perfectionism are we procrastinating? The people who want to achieve higher tend to do perfectly thus they get the outcomes very slowly. This “slower outcomes” lead to low self-esteem, depression, piling up of doable things, and finally giving up. Another downside of perfectionists is they constantly worry about making mistakes which makes them indecisive. There are two kinds of perfectionists – one who never starts, and the one who sets higher standards and is always failing. We all want to achieve things but there are multiple ways of procrastination as we have seen. What’s the solution for this perfectionism induced procrastination?  Since, this life is always about outcomes, we need to get tangible outcomes regularly. If we consider the opposite of a perfectionist, a slacker always has the attitude of not caring. However, that will never take us anywhere. Therefore, we need a middle ground. “Learned resourcefulness”. Goals can be counterproductive – when you make a mistake a perfectionist says, “This is the worst thing” and a slacker says, “I don’t care”. Therefore, to achieve goals we need to rely on systems and problem-solving skills. Always say “I’ve got this”.

How to read 100 Books a Year

Reading books is a good habit as, learning from others’ experiences is wiser than learning from own experiences. Therefore, buy books in bulk, always be reading, importantly the books that are relevant to you (ongoing life, profession, hobby, etc.). It’s also important to read multiple books simultaneously as some books might be information heavy (read in the daytime) and we tend to read them slowly, so reading books like biographies or fiction in the evening keeps you in the flow. Finally, what good is the time and money invested on books if we do not retain the knowledge and use it wisely. Therefore, highlight the key points, copy the quotes, etc. as you deem fit. But applying the knowledge in real life is the ultimate return of reading books.

Take a vacation: It boosts your productivity and reduces stress

Vacation is recharging your body and brain. It saves from burnout. So, what does a vacation mean? Resting with a specific reason. Continuous working is a sure path for burn out and an end to creative ideas. Instead, give into boredom, not distractions like Instagram, snapchat, etc., and find where your mind leads to. In general, we never find a good time to take a break – we are so busy trying to burnout. Take a break, get a life – life is a long- play it long.

One of the primary benefits of a genuine vacation is the opportunity it provides to relax, unwind, and reset. When you step away from your work responsibilities and daily routine, your mind gets a much-needed break. – Online source

Eliminate mindless browsing

Notifications are the key to distractions. We tend towards checking notifications because every time there is a notification there is a dopamine release. This addictive nature reduces the productivity and pushes us into multi-tasking. Multi-taskers are “suckers of irrelevancy” because they feel they are doing many things, but they are not achieving anything. Therefore, it is important to stop mindless browsing, multi-tasking and de-addict from notifications. Do what is important.

20 Things that will make your work productive

Productivity is about right mindset. We need it to achieve maximum output. Few things that matter and practicing them makes us productive are:

1.        Always cut to the chase – If you want to get shit done, you must jump straight into the action.

2.        Record all your thoughts and ideas – we may have elephants’ brain but still it is important to note things down as they help to expend less brain power trying to recollect.

3.        Say no – When in doubt say NO. Anything doesn’t support our goals and values, say NO!

4.        Take a 5-minute break every 30-45 minutes – Taking a break will help in re-evaluating our work, assessing if we are doing the right thing or wrong in timely manner saves lot of time.

5.        Eliminate everything that distracts you.

6.        Keep away clutter – Keep it simple.

7.        Focus on 1 thing some days – Try to do the recurring things in one day. This way we can focus on other things on remaining days.

8.        Stop consuming so much information – Stop consuming start creating. Apply what you have learnt.

9.        Create routines – Decisions fatigue your brain. Routines eliminate decisions. Use them.

10.  Don’t multitask – In research done by Gloria Mark of UC, Irvine, they showed that it takes an average of 25 minutes to return to original task after an interruption.

11.  Check e-mail twice a day – Dopamine is released when we check emails or notifications. Not that useful. Just a distraction and interruption.

12.  No smartphone during the first hour of your day – Enjoy your mornings. Think about the day ahead of you!

13.  Plan the next day – Take 5 to 10 minutes to plan the very important things for tomorrow. That way you know what to do. Otherwise, you will be working for others.

14.  Keep thinking to minimum – Don’t think too much. Start working and see how it goes.

15.  Exercise – Food water shelter relationships and exercise are vital. Exercise makes you happier, healthier and more energetic.

16.  Laugh a little – Laugh from cheek-to-cheek. No need to make a long face.

17.  Don’t go to meetings – unless important cut the crap.

18.  Is that really necessary – Always ask this question.

19.  Having a shitty day? press reset. Things go south but we have a routine in place. We have our problem-solving techniques at our disposal. Take a break. But don’t let a day go waste.

20.  And finally, it’s important to do the work – Talking about these things is easy. Just start working. If it doesn’t work, you know what to do.

 

Here's why time off work actually improves your work life

We are built to create things; therefore, we work. However, powering through work only leads to burnout and unoriginal thoughts/ideas. Common question: “If you love your work, why do you even want a break? – good point, smart ass”. In the book Elastic, Leonard Mlodinow demonstrates taking time off work improves well-being.

“Though some may consider “doing nothing” unproductive, a lack of downtime is bad for our well-being, because idle time allows our default network to make sense of what we’ve recently experienced or learned.”

Not taking time off means we are chasing short term goals and chasing short term goals leads to poor results, less productivity and sometime depression. (Even in Netflix culture report, they suggest one should refrain from actively pursuing short term goals.) Since prevention is better than cure, rest before fatigue hits you. Few advantages of taking a break involves: 1) Checking if we did the right things, 2) Process ideas – most of the time pursuing ideas is not great without giving much thought, 3) Consume more art – Art is not defined. Consuming art is inspiring. Suggested authors: Ernest Hemingway, Harper Lee, Ralph Ellison. 4) focus on other important things (not related to work) – hobbies, interests, etc. Show an initiative in building relationships. And finally, we realize that resting is boring – suggests that we are rejuvenated. And after working it is time for break not more work!!

Book reference – Elastic

 

Time Blocking: Improve your focus and get more meaningful work done

Yes, we all have goals, but we do not have time for them. As discussed earlier, planning the next day, in the same philosophical lines we should plan your weekly, quarterly and yearly goals. Why? To ensure progress is being made on the right things at the right pace for the relevant deadlines. Note that working is not progress, getting tangible results is progress. So, one way to focus to block time to get meaningful things done. Take 10 minutes every day to block the time. Don’t over schedule but allow more time than you think you need. Achieving meaningful things is hard but consistency is the key to do so with ease. Amateur works when inspiration strikes but a pro sits down every day and puts in steady work. Be a pro.

Book reference: The war of art.

I stopped working out daily. Hers’s what happened

Working out is the only way to strengthen body. It is the door to healthy life. Do not neglect it. Without a healthy body productivity suffers. “Motivation does not long enough, neither does bath- that’s why it is recommended daily” same goes to working out.

If you want to be more productive, research shows you need a break

Don’t waste time. Identify time wasters, eliminate them. More importantly, it is important to work in 30-minute intervals followed by a 5-minute break. De-activating and re-activating work allow you to stay focused. As we assess our work regularly, it will increase the quality of our work as well. Use the break to stretch, walk a bit, etc. Set a 30-minute interval. Take 15-minute break after 4 intervals. Set a daily goal. Don’t accept interruptions or false emergencies. No matter whatever you do – take a 5-minute break!

Part III: Achieving More

Don’t know what you want? Improve these 7 universal skills

If we know what we want from life, it’s good. If not, now we know that figuring out what we want from this life is important – without panicking. However, it is the most difficult thing. No one knows the nitty-gritty details of everything that is needed from life, however, a direction or the bigger picture should be known. While we are figuring out what we want with this life, work on these universal skills: Self-Discipline, Personal effectiveness, Communication, Negotiation, Persuasion, Physical strength and Stamina, and Flexibility. Pick a skill, work on it. Repeat the process if not clear yet!

Book reference: Getting Everything You Can Out of All You’ve Got

Leave the office on time and don’t take your work home

It is practically impossible for many of the people to say, “I finished my top priorities today; I am going home.” Why? We don’t want to look bad. But why are we working in the first place? We want to contribute effectively and productively. By working overtime we are reducing your productivity, becoming unhealthy, etc. Therefore, two rules, first: leave the office on time – if not, we are either being unproductive or you are just burdening others by staying late in the office. Second: don’t take work home – we know that life is bigger than all the work we do. What if you die today? Spend time with your loved ones. Don’t fret about work at home. Work is work. Do the work you love but don’t overdo - It spoils the fun.

Consistency is Key: Improve by 0.1% Every day

When things go wrong, we become our worst enemies because we worry about things that we do not control – happens to all. At the end of the day, we can control only our actions and mindset. Desire, Attitude, Judgements, and Determination – we can control. What counts is that we do right things and that is all we can control. Made a mistake? Correct it. Something went wrong. Find a solution. The philosophy of being productive and improving is consistency. Every single day. Productivity is not about eureka moments, and more importantly not about worrying but consistent improvement by 0.1%. Inconsistency is the enemy of all productivity.

Who says you have to get out of your comfort zone?

Anybody who asks us to get out of our comfort zone is wrong. There is only misery when we are out of our comfort zone – not necessary. We are not going to find a bag of gold when we get out of our comfort zone. Sometimes we prefer to stay in the comfort zone and work upwards, and sometimes we like to go all out and take risks. It is the preparedness that allows us to try new things. If you want a stress-free life, all one should have a strong foundation. A fail-safe system in place. It is always best to have enough money in savings. If you do not have money don’t even think of taking risks. Build skills. Other things that include foundation are family- don’t have? create one, friends-what is life without friends that don’t stick around, and yourself – invest in personal growth. And lastly, introvert or extrovert, be it - no need to make life miserable by not being ourselves. Push limits slowly. That’s all needed.

If you can believe it, you can achieve it

Everything that is floating in our head is because we believe it. A pragmatist thinks like this, “you will never become a respectable leader without putting in the work”; “your life will never change unless you act”. Simply put, we must believe to achieve it. It’s one thing to say that we believe it’s on a whole different level to actually believe it. We always make decisions based on what we believe. For example, we do a job that we hate because we fear we might lose our house. That’s what we believe. Reframe the perspective to “I can keep the house while doing the job I like.” And suddenly we find solutions for that. Just believe that we can do it.

Don’t Compete. Create.

Life is abundant. The opportunities are many. Can’t find the right career, create one. It doesn’t matter what others think. If we believe that we can create value, go for it. There are always people who says that “no one needs this”, “your work is garbage”, “you are wasting time”, etc. Let them drown in their own limiting beliefs. And work on creating. Adopt the mindset of abundance. There are enough opportunities in the world. Use it.

All strength comes from repetition

Stick to basics. We have heard this so many times. And what does this mean? According to Daniel Comier “you don’t get to the highest levels of the sport without having the basics in order”. “Long term we have to build internal strength”. How to do this? We need to repeat basics – to allow ourselves to fail, and that’s when we become more aware of our weaknesses and strengths. Muhammed Ali said that “It’s the repetition of affirmations that becomes belief and once this belief become deep conviction, things begin to happen”. When you repeat basics, you don’t only become great, you stay great. It’s a game of long term. Play smart. Repeat basics. Become strong. And finally, don’t repeat anything bad, wrong or negative things and most importantly don’t take advice from people who haven’t done something great themselves.

How I measure my Life

Measuring business is easy as there are numbers like balance sheet, profits, etc. To measure life, it is up to oneself. However, most successful people – in the eyes of the society- measure life in three factors. Energy, Work and Relationships. Measuring energy is easy: How you feel physically? Exercise regularly, eat healthy. The more energetic you are, you will work more/ productively, you will give more and leading to happier life. Measuring work is tricky as most people look at income – which is not incorrect – but it is the meaningful (to you) work that should bring income. The more you learn and apply it, the more you are given responsibilities – more contribution - leading to more income. Measuring relationships: We need to have relationships that we can count on. That will complete the life’s circle. The more we contribute to other’s life the better relationship will be as a result. One caution is that we should not measure a relationship by looking at what others are doing for us. It is up to them. The relationship improves when we make time for the people who matters to us. If it did not, the relationship was never meant to be. Our energy, our work and the effort in our relationship are the things that we can control - focus on that. This is what all the philosophers all over the world from all ages said.

Book reference: How will you measure your life

Why you should live like you are immortal

“Before you know it, you will be dead” – is the mindset to get started. But, once we start, what shall keep us going? The bigger picture. If we are chasing every shiny object, we will be achieving nothing at the end. Once we get started, think that we have forever – immortal life. Now we can do meaningful things that take some time to yield results. Otherwise, we are chasing shiny things in life without actually putting efforts to build something meaningful. Be satisfied with the present using all the techniques we have discussed, while also looking at the bigger picture. As it was said, there is nothing sadder than realizing that we put our efforts into something wrong at the age of 70.

Book reference: Meditations by Marcus Aurelius

The power of compounding: You can achieve anything if you stop trying everything

Success is sequential not simultaneous. Nobody can do everything at the same time. Therefore, success builds up sequentially. Put in the work regularly, focus on one thing at a time, and always look at the bigger picture. Success comes its way. Who cares when. It will happen, that’s all that matters.

Book reference: The One Thing – Gary Keller and Jay Papasan
